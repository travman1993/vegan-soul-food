Smoky BBQ Pulled Jackfruit Sandwiches

Serves 4‚Äì6 ¬∑ Prep 15 min ¬∑ Cook 25 min ¬∑ 100% Vegan

Tender, pulled jackfruit simmered in smoky-sweet BBQ sauce creates the perfect plant-based alternative to pulled pork. Pile it high on soft buns with tangy coleslaw for an authentic soul food sandwich experience.

Ingredients
  • 2 cans (20 oz each) young green jackfruit in water or brine, drained
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup BBQ sauce (your favorite brand)
  • 1/4 cup vegetable broth (low sodium)
  • 2 tbsp apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tbsp maple syrup or brown sugar
  • 2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tsp liquid smoke
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Soft hamburger buns for serving
  • Coleslaw and pickles for topping
How to Prepare
  1. Drain and rinse jackfruit. Pat dry and shred with your hands or two forks, removing any tough cores or seeds.
  2. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add diced onion and saut√© 5‚Äì7 minutes until softened and lightly caramelized.
  3. Add garlic and cook 1 minute until fragrant.
  4. Add shredded jackfruit to the skillet along with smoked paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, and cayenne. Stir well to coat.
  5. Cook 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until jackfruit begins to brown slightly.
  6. Add BBQ sauce, vegetable broth, apple cider vinegar, maple syrup, and liquid smoke. Stir to combine.
  7. Reduce heat to medium-low and simmer 15‚Äì20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until jackfruit is tender and sauce has thickened. Use a fork or potato masher to further shred the jackfruit for that authentic pulled texture.
  8. Season with salt and black pepper to taste.
  9. Serve hot on toasted buns topped with coleslaw and pickles.

Tip: For deeper smoky flavor, add an extra 1/2 tsp liquid smoke or grill the jackfruit mixture on foil over medium heat for 5 minutes after cooking. This also reheats beautifully and tastes even better the next day.

Vegan Jambalaya (New Orleans Style)

Serves 6‚Äì8 ¬∑ Prep 20 min ¬∑ Cook 40 min ¬∑ 100% Vegan

A one-pot Louisiana classic loaded with rice, vegetables, kidney beans, and bold Creole flavors. This plant-based version delivers all the spicy, smoky satisfaction of traditional jambalaya without any meat or seafood.

Ingredients
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 3 celery stalks, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 8 oz vegan sausage, sliced (optional but recommended)
  • 1 1/2 cups long-grain white rice
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes with juice
  • 1 can (15 oz) kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 3 cups vegetable broth (low sodium)
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 2 tsp Cajun or Creole seasoning
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tsp liquid smoke (optional)
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 4 green onions, sliced
  • Fresh parsley, chopped
  • Hot sauce for serving
How to Prepare
  1. Heat olive oil in a large heavy pot or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add vegan sausage and cook 3‚Äì4 minutes until browned. Remove and set aside.
  2. In the same pot, add onion, bell peppers, and celery. Saut√© 6‚Äì8 minutes until softened and starting to caramelize.
  3. Add garlic and cook 1 minute until fragrant.
  4. Stir in Cajun seasoning, smoked paprika, thyme, and cayenne. Cook 1 minute to toast the spices.
  5. Add rice and stir for 1‚Äì2 minutes to coat with seasonings.
  6. Pour in diced tomatoes, kidney beans, vegetable broth, bay leaves, and liquid smoke if using. Return sausage to pot.
  7. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low. Cover and simmer 20‚Äì25 minutes until rice is tender and liquid is absorbed. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.
  8. Remove bay leaves. Season with salt and black pepper to taste.
  9. Let stand covered for 5 minutes, then fluff with a fork.
  10. Garnish with green onions and fresh parsley. Serve hot with your favorite hot sauce.

Tip: For extra vegetables, add sliced okra or corn during the last 10 minutes of cooking. Jambalaya tastes even better the next day after flavors have melded.

New Orleans-Style Vegan Red Beans & Rice

Serves 6‚Äì8 ¬∑ Prep 15 min ¬∑ Cook 1 hr 15 min ¬∑ 100% Vegan

A Monday night tradition in New Orleans made completely plant-based. Creamy red beans simmered low and slow with the holy trinity, Creole spices, and smoky seasonings served over fluffy white rice. Rich, hearty, and soul-satisfying.

Ingredients
  • 1 lb dried red kidney beans, soaked overnight and drained
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 3 celery stalks, diced
  • 5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 6 cups vegetable broth (low sodium)
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 2 tsp Creole seasoning
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tsp liquid smoke
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 4 cups cooked white rice
  • 4 green onions, sliced
  • Fresh parsley, chopped
  • Hot sauce for serving
How to Prepare
  1. Heat olive oil in a large heavy pot over medium heat. Add onion, bell pepper, and celery. Saut√© 7‚Äì8 minutes until softened and starting to caramelize.
  2. Add garlic and cook 1 minute until fragrant.
  3. Stir in tomato paste, Creole seasoning, smoked paprika, thyme, oregano, and cayenne. Cook 1‚Äì2 minutes to deepen flavors.
  4. Add drained beans, vegetable broth, bay leaves, and liquid smoke. Bring to a boil.
  5. Reduce heat to low, cover partially, and simmer 1 hour, stirring occasionally.
  6. After 1 hour, beans should be tender. Use a potato masher or back of a spoon to mash about 1/3 of the beans against the side of the pot. This creates that signature creamy texture.
  7. Continue cooking uncovered 10‚Äì15 minutes until mixture thickens to your liking.
  8. Remove bay leaves. Season with salt and black pepper to taste.
  9. Serve over white rice, garnished with green onions and fresh parsley. Pass hot sauce at the table.

Tip: Red beans taste even better on day two and three as flavors meld. This freezes beautifully for make-ahead meals. Traditionally served on Mondays in New Orleans using Sunday's leftover ham bones, but this vegan version needs no meat to deliver authentic flavor.

Vegan Mustard Greens & White Beans

Serves 4‚Äì6 ¬∑ Prep 15 min ¬∑ Cook 30 min ¬∑ 100% Vegan

Tender mustard greens simmered with creamy white beans in a flavorful, peppery broth. This soul food classic is packed with nutrients and delivers bold, satisfying flavor with every bite.

Ingredients
  • 2 large bunches mustard greens, stems removed and chopped
  • 2 cans (15 oz each) great northern or cannellini beans, drained and rinsed
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp red pepper flakes (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 3 cups vegetable broth (low sodium)
  • 2 tbsp apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tsp liquid smoke (optional)
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Hot sauce for serving
How to Prepare
  1. Wash mustard greens thoroughly and chop into bite-sized pieces, discarding tough stems.
  2. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add onion and saut√© 5‚Äì7 minutes until softened and translucent.
  3. Add garlic, smoked paprika, red pepper flakes, and thyme. Cook 1 minute until fragrant.
  4. Add chopped mustard greens in batches, stirring until they begin to wilt.
  5. Pour in vegetable broth and bring to a boil. Reduce heat to medium-low and simmer 15‚Äì20 minutes until greens are tender.
  6. Stir in white beans and cook another 5‚Äì7 minutes until heated through and flavors meld.
  7. Add apple cider vinegar and liquid smoke if using. Season with salt and black pepper to taste.
  8. Serve hot with cornbread and pass hot sauce at the table.

Tip: Mustard greens have a natural peppery bite that mellows with cooking. For a milder flavor, substitute half the mustard greens with collards or turnip greens. This dish tastes even better the next day.

Skillet Okra & Tomatoes (Southern Style)

Serves 4‚Äì6 ¬∑ Prep 10 min ¬∑ Cook 25 min ¬∑ 100% Vegan

A classic Southern side dish featuring tender okra cooked down with juicy tomatoes, onions, and savory seasonings. This comforting vegan version captures all the home-cooked flavor of traditional soul food kitchens.

Ingredients
  • 1 lb fresh okra, sliced into 1/2-inch rounds
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes with juice
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp dried thyme
  • 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 tbsp apple cider vinegar
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup vegetable broth (low sodium)
  • Fresh parsley for garnish
How to Prepare
  1. Heat olive oil in a large cast iron skillet or heavy pot over medium-high heat.
  2. Add diced onion and bell pepper. Saut√© 5‚Äì7 minutes until softened and lightly caramelized.
  3. Add garlic and cook 1 minute until fragrant.
  4. Stir in sliced okra and cook 5 minutes, stirring occasionally to reduce the natural sliminess.
  5. Add diced tomatoes with juice, vegetable broth, smoked paprika, thyme, cayenne, and bay leaf.
  6. Season with salt and black pepper. Bring to a simmer.
  7. Reduce heat to medium-low, cover, and cook 12‚Äì15 minutes until okra is tender but not mushy.
  8. Stir in apple cider vinegar. Remove bay leaf.
  9. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve hot over rice or with cornbread.

Tip: For less "slimy" okra, cook it over higher heat initially to evaporate moisture, or add a squeeze of lemon juice at the end. This dish tastes even better the next day.

Hearty Vegan Gumbo

Serves 6‚Äì8 ¬∑ Prep 25 min ¬∑ Cook 50 min ¬∑ 100% Vegan

A thick, richly flavored Louisiana classic loaded with okra, kidney beans, and the holy trinity of vegetables. This plant-based version starts with a dark roux and delivers all the soul and complexity of traditional gumbo.

Ingredients
  • 1/2 cup neutral oil
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 3 celery stalks, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups fresh or frozen okra, sliced
  • 1 can (15 oz) kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 6 cups vegetable broth (low sodium)
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 2 tsp Cajun seasoning
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tsp file powder (optional, added at end)
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 4 green onions, sliced
  • Fresh parsley, chopped
  • Cooked white rice for serving
  • Hot sauce for serving
How to Prepare
  1. Make the roux: In a large heavy-bottomed pot, heat oil over medium heat. Gradually whisk in flour. Continue whisking constantly for 15‚Äì20 minutes until the roux turns a deep chocolate brown. Be patient ‚Äì this is the flavor foundation.
  2. Carefully add onion, bell pepper, and celery to the hot roux (it will sizzle). Stir constantly for 5‚Äì7 minutes until vegetables soften.
  3. Add garlic and okra, cooking 3‚Äì4 minutes more.
  4. Stir in diced tomatoes, kidney beans, vegetable broth, bay leaves, Cajun seasoning, smoked paprika, thyme, and cayenne. Bring to a boil.
  5. Reduce heat to low and simmer uncovered 30‚Äì35 minutes, stirring occasionally, until gumbo thickens and flavors meld.
  6. Remove from heat. Discard bay leaves. Stir in file powder if using (don't boil after adding file as it gets stringy).
  7. Season with salt and black pepper to taste.
  8. Serve hot over white rice, garnished with green onions and parsley. Pass hot sauce at the table.

Tip: Gumbo tastes even better the next day after flavors have had time to develop. Store in the fridge up to 5 days or freeze for longer storage.

Vegan Dirty Rice (Cajun-Style)

A Louisiana classic made completely plant-based. Savory rice cooked with the "holy trinity" of Cajun cooking, plenty of spices, and hearty mushrooms and lentils for that meaty texture. Full of soul, no meat required.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups vegetable broth (low sodium)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil

  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp dried thyme
  • 1/2 tsp oregano
  • 1/4 tsp cayenne pepper (adjust to heat preference)
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 3 green onions, sliced (for garnish)
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)
  • Hot sauce for serving
How to Prepare
  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot or deep skillet over medium-high heat. Add onion, bell pepper, and celery (the "holy trinity"). Saut√© 5‚Äì7 minutes until softened.
  2. Add chopped mushrooms and cook another 5 minutes until they release moisture and begin to brown.

  5. Add rice and stir for 1‚Äì2 minutes to toast slightly.
  6. Pour in vegetable broth and add bay leaves. Bring to a boil.
  7. Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer 15‚Äì18 minutes until rice is tender and liquid is absorbed.
  8. Stir in cooked lentils and let sit covered for 5 minutes off heat.
  9. Remove bay leaves, fluff with a fork, and garnish with green onions and parsley.

  5. Pour batter into prepared pan and smooth the top.

Vegan Fried "Chicken" (Crispy Oyster Mushroom Style)

Serves 3‚Äì4 ¬∑ Prep 20 min ¬∑ Cook 20 min ¬∑ 100% Vegan

All the crunch, none of the chicken. Meaty oyster mushrooms get seasoned, battered, and fried until golden ‚Äì perfect with hot sauce and a side of greens. Completely plant-based, totally delicious.

Ingredients
  • 1 lb oyster mushrooms (or large king oyster pieces)
  • 1 cup unsweetened plant milk (soy or oat)
  • 1 tbsp apple cider vinegar
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch
  • 2 tsp garlic powder
  • 2 tsp onion powder
  • 2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 tsp salt + more to taste
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp cayenne (optional for heat)
  • Neutral oil for frying
How to Prepare
  1. In a bowl, mix the plant milk and apple cider vinegar. Let sit 5 minutes to curdle slightly ‚Äì this creates a vegan buttermilk substitute.
  2. Gently clean the mushrooms and tear into large "chicken" pieces.
  3. In another bowl, whisk together flour, cornstarch, garlic powder, onion powder, smoked paprika, thyme, salt, pepper, and cayenne.
  4. Dip each mushroom piece into the plant milk mixture, then into the flour mix, pressing to coat. For extra crunch, dip back in milk mixture and flour once more.
  5. Heat 1‚Äì2 inches of oil in a heavy skillet to about 350¬∞F (medium-high). Fry mushrooms in batches 3‚Äì4 minutes per side until deep golden and crispy.
  6. Transfer to a wire rack or paper towel, sprinkle with a little more salt, and serve hot.

Tip: Leftovers crisp back up in an air fryer or hot oven.

Vegan Soul Food Cooking Tips & FAQs

New to plant-based Southern cooking? These quick tips help you get the best flavor, texture, and results from every vegan soul food recipe.

How do you add smoky flavor without meat?

Use smoked paprika, liquid smoke, fire-roasted tomatoes, and caramelized onions. These ingredients recreate the depth traditionally added by smoked meats.

What‚Äôs the best way to prep greens?

Wash greens thoroughly to remove grit, then remove thick stems. Slow simmering in seasoned vegetable broth softens bitterness and builds rich flavor.

How do you prevent soggy or slimy okra?

Cook okra over higher heat first to evaporate moisture, or saut√© before simmering. Adding acidic ingredients like vinegar or tomatoes helps reduce slime.

What makes vegan fried ‚Äúchicken‚Äù extra crispy?

Double-dip oyster mushrooms in seasoned batter and let them rest 5 minutes before frying. Fry at 350¬∞F for a golden, crunchy crust.

How do you thicken beans and stews naturally?

Mash a portion of the beans against the pot or blend a small scoop and stir it back in. This creates a creamy texture without flour or dairy.

Can vegan soul food be meal-prepped?

Absolutely. Dishes like red beans & rice, greens, gumbo, and yams taste even better the next day and freeze well for easy meals.

What plant-based ingredients replace meat best?

Jackfruit, oyster mushrooms, lentils, and beans provide hearty texture and absorb seasoning well‚Äîperfect for Southern-style cooking.

How do you balance flavors in vegan comfort food?

Use a balance of fat, acid, salt, and heat. Vinegar or lemon brightens dishes, while spices and slow cooking deepen flavor.
